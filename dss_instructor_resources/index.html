OVERVIEW OF MY COURSE

My course progresses through bite-size exercises that students engage with at least three times: first with the textbook, then in class, and again in the weekly take-home problem sets. All three-textbook, in-class exercises, and problem sets-move in parallel, drawing on the same statistical concepts and code but asking students to answer different research questions using distinct real-world datasets. This gives students repeated hands-on experience and helps them practice applying quantitative reasoning across a variety of contexts.

  • Logistics: Meets twice a week for 75 minutes per session, over the span of 12-13 weeks. There are no teaching assistants, but several former students offer one-on-one tutoring and lead optional study group sessions.
  • Content: It skips some of the more advanced topics in DSS because it is designed for undergraduate students with no prior experience in coding or statistics, and only minimal math background.
  • My syllabus is here.
  • All code and datasets used in the book are available in the DSS folder here.
  • To share resources with your students, direct them to the DSS Student Resources Website, which contains all student-facing materials including self-graded review exercises, interactive graphs, videos, and additional readings.
