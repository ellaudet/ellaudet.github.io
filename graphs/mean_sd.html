 Mean and Standard Deviation

By Elena Llaudet, co-author of Data Analysis for Social Science (DSS)

The mean determines the center of a distribution. The standard deviation determines its spreadâ€”roughly, the average distance from each observation to the mean.

Let's see how changing these parameters affects the distribution of a variable:

STEP 1: The graph below shows the distribution of a normal random variable with mean 0 and standard deviation 1. (Note: We use a normal random variable as our illustration, but these concepts apply to others.) The mean centers the curve at zero; the standard deviation makes the observations be, on average, roughly 1 unit away from the mean.
STEP 2: Use the first slider to change the mean. Notice that this shifts the distribution left or right without changing its shape or spread.
STEP 3: Use the second slider to change the standard deviation. Notice that larger standard deviations create wider, flatter distributions, because the observations are, on average, further away from the mean. By contrast, smaller standard deviations create narrower, taller distributions, because the observations are, on average, closer to the mean. However, the center stays the same.
